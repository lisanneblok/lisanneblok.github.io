About me

Introduction

PhD researcher at the university of Cambridge as part of the AI4ER CDT. I am passionate about sea level rise and extreme events, demonstrated by the need to keep my house from flooding in the Netherlands.

I use AI, mostly Graph Neural Networks and probabilistic ML to capture patterns in the sea level signal, to uncover correlations between causations of extreme sea level events on big and small scales. I aim to inform policy makers of the risks of the sea and am hoping to make a positive impact.

In my free time, I am an avid sailor, skiier and runner!

Research projects

Extreme sea level

Understanding the extreme sea level signal and corresponding uncertainties using the GESLA3 dataset, ERA5, CoDEC model.

Arctic Turbulence.

XGBoost regression on Arctic and global microstructure data, preedicting ocean kinetic turbulence, to infer mixing patterns in a changing climate.

Social inequality for the Social Cost of Carbon under Risk aversion.

Using the DICE and RICE model to understand social cost of carbon under different economic systems.
